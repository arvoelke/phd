\chapter*{Mathematical Conventions}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Mathematical Conventions}
\label{typography}

This thesis follows the standard mathematical conventions of using lower-case italic variables ($u(t)$) to represent scalar quantities, lower-case bold variables ($\V{x}(t)$) to represent vector quantities, and upper-case italic variables ($A$) to represent matrices.
Depending on context, $F(s)$ or $F(z)$ are used to represent complex-values in the Laplace $s$-domain, or $z$-domain, respectively.
